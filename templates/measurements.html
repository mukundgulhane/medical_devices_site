Pulse & Foot Pressure Measurement Circuits

Pulse Measurement (Heart Rate Monitoring)

Pulse measurement works on the principle of Photoplethysmography (PPG), a non-invasive optical technique used in medical devices such as pulse oximeters, smartwatch health trackers, and clinical patient monitoring systems.

Key Components

  • LED (Red or IR): Emits light through the fingertip. Blood absorbs this light during each heartbeat.
  • Photodiode/Phototransistor: Detects the reflected light coming from changes in blood volume.
  • Op-Amp (LM358/LM324): Amplifies the small PPG signal and removes noise.
  • Low-Pass Filter: Smoothens the signal to show the pulse waveform clearly.
  • Microcontroller (Arduino/ESP32): Reads the analog values, processes peaks, and calculates BPM.
  • Display (LCD/OLED): Shows the measured heart rate.

Mechanism (How PPG Works)

The LED shines light into the fingertip ‚Üí blood absorbs light. During each heartbeat:

Systole (Heart pumps): More blood flows ‚Üí more absorption ‚Üí less light reaches photodiode. Diastole (Heart relaxes): Blood volume reduces ‚Üí less absorption ‚Üí more reflected light detected.

These rising and falling light levels create a pulse waveform that represents heartbeats.


Detailed Explanation (Medical + Electronics)

  • Biological Aspect: Blood volume oscillates with every heartbeat; this optical change becomes the PPG signal.
  • Signal Conditioning: Raw photodiode output is in microvolts ‚Üí Op-Amp amplifies 20‚Äì200√ó.
  • Filtering: A low-pass filter removes finger motion and electric noise.
  • Peak Detection: Microcontroller identifies rising edges or peaks to count beats per minute (BPM).
  • Output: BPM = Number of peaks in 10 seconds √ó 6 (or rolling average).

Foot Pressure Measurement (FSR-Based)

Foot Pressure Measurement

Foot pressure measurement is used in medical rehabilitation, gait analysis, prosthetics, and wearable health monitoring. It operates using an FSR (Force Sensitive Resistor), which converts applied force into a change in electrical resistance.

Key Components

  • FSR / Pressure Sensor: Resistance decreases as pressure increases, giving a measurable output.
  • Voltage Divider: Converts FSR resistance changes into measurable voltage.
  • Op-Amp (Optional): Amplifies the small voltage for better resolution.
  • Microcontroller (Arduino/ESP32): Reads analog voltage through ADC and converts it to force/pressure.
  • LCD/OLED Display: Shows pressure value, gait pattern, or load distribution.

Mechanism (How Foot Pressure is Detected)

When a person steps on the FSR:

Low pressure: High resistance ‚Üí low output voltage.
High pressure: Low resistance ‚Üí higher voltage output.

The microcontroller reads these voltage variations through its ADC, maps them to force/pressure values, and displays them in real-time.


Detailed Explanation (Medical + Electronics)

  • Material Behavior: FSR has conductive particles that compress under force, lowering resistance.
  • Voltage Divider Role: Converts non-linear FSR resistance into measurable voltage.
  • Signal Conditioning: Optional op-amp improves sensitivity for low-pressure applications.
  • ADC Conversion: Microcontroller maps 0‚Äì1023 (Arduino) or 0‚Äì4095 (ESP32) to pressure units.
  • Calibration: Known weights are used to create a pressure-force mapping curve.
