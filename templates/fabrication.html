1. Microfluidic Devices: Miniaturizing Diagnostics

**Microfluidic devices** are used to manipulate small fluid volumes (micro to nano liters) for applications in diagnostics, chemical synthesis, and biological analysis. These devices are often called Lab-on-a-Chip systems because they integrate multiple laboratory functions on a single chip.

Fabrication via PDMS and Laser Printing

  • PDMS (Polydimethylsiloxane): Flexible polymer widely used for rapid prototyping of microfluidic channels.
  • Laser Printing: Used to pattern the master mold for casting PDMS channels.

Simplified Fabrication Steps:

  1. Design channel layout using CAD software.
  2. Laser print or etch the mold pattern on a substrate.
  3. Pour PDMS over the mold, degas to remove bubbles, and cure.
  4. Bond the PDMS chip to glass or another polymer substrate.

2. Lateral Flow Assay (LFA)

A Lateral Flow Assay is a paper-based diagnostic platform that uses capillary action to allow fluid samples to flow across various reagents. LFAs are widely used for rapid diagnostic tests like pregnancy tests, infectious disease detection, and point-of-care tests.

Basic Components:

  • Sample Pad: Receives and filters the sample.
  • Conjugate Pad: Contains labeled detection antibodies or probes.
  • Nitrocellulose Membrane: Reaction zone with test (T) and control (C) lines.
  • Absorbent Pad: Maintains continuous capillary flow.

Working Principle:

  1. Sample absorbs into the conjugate pad, picking up labeled detection probes.
  2. Sample-probe complex migrates along the nitrocellulose membrane to the Test Line (T-Line).
  3. Excess fluid continues to the Control Line (C-Line) to validate the test.

3. Ergonomics and Human Factors in IVD Device Design

Ergonomics, or Human Factors Engineering, is crucial for IVD devices. Poorly designed devices can lead to sample handling errors, misinterpretation of results, or user discomfort. The goal is to design devices and workflows that fit the user and reduce errors.

Ergonomic PrincipleFocus AreaIVD Device Application
Physical ErgonomicsAnatomy & BiomechanicsComfortable sampling tools and pipettes to reduce fatigue
Cognitive ErgonomicsMental ProcessesClear visual cues, simple workflow, and readable displays
Organizational ErgonomicsSystems & WorkflowLogical packaging, instructions, and disposal procedures
  • High-contrast LFA test window to minimize reading errors.
  • Distinct reagent vial caps and labels to avoid mix-ups.
  • Simple digital feedback on electronic readers for confirmation.
