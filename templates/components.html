Introduction to Components & Circuit Design - Medical Devices

Resistor

Limits current, drops voltage. Used in current limiting for LEDs, ECG circuits.

Unit: Ohm (Œ©)

Diode

Allows current in one direction. Types: standard, LED, Zener. Used in protection circuits.

Inductor

Stores energy in magnetic field; filters AC signals. Used in ECG signal smoothing.

Capacitor

Stores energy in electric field; blocks DC, passes AC. Used for filtering noise.

LED

Light indicator. Shows device status, power ON/OFF.

Switch

Controls ON/OFF state of circuits. Used in medical devices to start/stop instruments.

Small Circuit Example

LED indicator for a pulse sensor circuit.

+5V
|
[Switch]
|
[Resistor]
|
[LED]----GND

Explanation:

  • Switch: Turns device ON/OFF.
  • Resistor: Limits current to LED.
  • LED: Lights up when power is ON.
  • Optional Diode: Protects LED from reverse polarity.

Medical Device Note: Used in pulse oximeters, ECG front-end indicators, infusion pumps.
