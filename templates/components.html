Introduction to Components & Circuit Design - Medical Devices

Resistor

Limits current, drops voltage. Used in current limiting for LEDs, ECG circuits.

Unit: Ohm (Œ©)

A resistor is a fundamental electronic component widely used in medical devices to control current and voltage.

  • Limits electric current in a circuit.
  • Ensures correct voltage for sensitive components.
  • Protects sensors and microcontrollers from damage.
  • Used in signal conditioning and biasing circuits.
  • Critical for accurate readings in ECG, pulse oximeters, infusion pumps.
  • Helps maintain device safety and reliability.
  • Available in various types: fixed, variable, and precision resistors.

How to Read a Resistor

Resistors are marked with colored bands to indicate their value and tolerance. Follow these steps:

  • Identify the color bands on the resistor (usually 4 or 5 bands).
  • Read the first two (or three) bands as digits.
  • The next band is the multiplier (power of 10).
  • The last band shows tolerance (accuracy of the resistor).
  • Use a resistor color code chart to determine the exact value.
  • Ensure correct resistor selection for medical circuits to avoid errors.

Diode

A diode is an electronic component that allows current to flow in only one direction, providing control and protection in circuits.

  • Allows current to flow in one direction only (forward-biased).
  • Blocks current in reverse direction (reverse-biased).
  • Used for **rectification** in power supplies of medical devices.
  • Protects circuits from voltage spikes and reverse polarity.
  • Essential for sensors, microcontrollers, and signal protection.
  • Available types: LED, Zener, Schottky, and standard diodes.

Inductor

An inductor is a passive electronic component that stores energy in a magnetic field when current flows through it, widely used in medical electronics.

  • Stores energy in a magnetic field.
  • Opposes sudden changes in current (acts as a filter).
  • Used in **signal filtering** and noise reduction in medical devices.
  • Critical for ECG, pulse sensors, and power supply circuits.
  • Types include air-core, iron-core, and toroidal inductors.
  • Helps maintain stable operation of sensitive circuits.

Capacitor

A capacitor is an electronic component that stores and releases electrical energy, essential for medical and electronic circuits.

  • Stores electrical charge temporarily.
  • Used for **filtering, smoothing, and decoupling** in circuits.
  • Helps stabilize voltage in sensitive medical devices.
  • Essential in ECG, pulse oximeters, and sensor circuits.
  • Types include ceramic, electrolytic, tantalum, and film capacitors.
  • Improves performance and reliability of electronic systems.

LED (Light Emitting Diode)

An LED is a diode that emits light when current passes through it, commonly used for indicators and displays in medical devices.

  • Emits light when forward-biased due to **electron-hole recombination** in the semiconductor material.
  • Electron drops from the conduction band to the valence band, releasing energy as photons (light).
  • Available in different colors; blue LEDs are harder to produce because they require **wide bandgap semiconductors** (like GaN) and precise fabrication.
  • Used in medical devices like pulse oximeters and display panels.
  • Low power consumption and long lifespan.
  • Must be connected with a current-limiting resistor to prevent damage.
  • Can also be used in optical sensors and signaling applications.

Switch

A switch is an electrical component that can open or close a circuit, controlling the flow of current in medical and electronic devices.

  • Controls current flow by making (ON) or breaking (OFF) the circuit.
  • Types include toggle, push-button, slide, and rotary switches.
  • Examples in medical devices: ON/OFF switch in ECG machines, push-button in infusion pumps, rotary switch in ventilators.
  • Ensures safety by allowing the device to be turned off quickly.
  • Can be mechanical or electronic (solid-state switches).
  • Used in electronics: controlling LED panels, power to microcontrollers, or activating alarms.

Potentiometer (Variable Resistor)

A potentiometer is a three-terminal resistor with a sliding or rotating contact that adjusts resistance. It is used to control voltage and signal levels in circuits.

  • Adjusts voltage in a circuit by changing resistance.
  • Used for **calibration and tuning** in medical devices.
  • Examples: adjusting LED brightness, sensor sensitivity, or audio volume in medical equipment.
  • Three terminals: two fixed ends and one wiper (adjustable middle terminal).
  • Common types: rotary, slide, and multi-turn potentiometers.
  • Essential for precise control in ECG machines, infusion pumps, and ventilator sensors.
  • Can act as a voltage divider when needed in electronic circuits.

7-Segment Display

A 7-segment display is used to show numeric data visually in electronic circuits.

  • Used for displaying heart rate, temperature, or sensor readings.
  • Commonly driven using microcontrollers or BCD decoders.
  • Easy to read, suitable for small portable medical devices.
  • Can be common cathode or common anode types.
  • Low power and compact for embedded devices.

LCD Display

Liquid Crystal Displays (LCD) show alphanumeric and graphical data in medical electronics.

  • Used in patient monitoring, pulse oximeters, and infusion pumps.
  • Can be character or graphical displays.
  • Low power and readable under various lighting conditions.
  • Interfaced via parallel or I2C/SPI with microcontrollers.
  • Improves visualization of complex measurements.

Transistors

Transistors act as switches or amplifiers in medical circuits.

  • NPN/PNP bipolar junction transistors amplify weak sensor signals.
  • MOSFETs used for power control or digital switching.
  • Used in ECG, pulse sensors, and microcontroller signal amplification.
  • Can be used to drive displays, buzzers, or actuators.
  • Critical for signal conditioning and protection of circuits.

Crystals & Oscillators

Provide precise timing reference for microcontrollers and devices.

  • Used in ECG/EEG data sampling for accurate readings.
  • Ensures microcontroller operates at correct frequency.
  • Common types: quartz crystals, ceramic resonators, MEMS oscillators.
  • Important for synchronization of digital medical devices.
  • Stable clock improves data reliability in sensors.
